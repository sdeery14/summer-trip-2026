## The Route at a Glance

The Greater Yellowstone Loop connects three world-class areas in a natural circuit through northwest Wyoming and southern Montana:

```
        ┌─── BOZEMAN (BZN) ───┐
        │                      │
   Yellowstone NP          Beartooth Hwy
   (north entrance)        (Red Lodge → Cooke City)
        │                      │
        └─── Yellowstone NP ───┘
             (interior)
                  │
            South to Grand Teton NP
                  │
           JACKSON HOLE (JAC)
```

The loop is roughly 350-450 miles depending on route choices, connecting:
- **Yellowstone National Park** — 2.2 million acres of geothermal wonders, wildlife, and backcountry
- **Beartooth Highway (US-212)** — 68 miles of America's most dramatic alpine highway, topping out at 10,947 ft
- **Grand Teton National Park** — cathedral-like peaks rising 7,000 ft above the valley floor, world-class hiking

This is a natural multi-park loop — similar in structure to your Yosemite + Sequoia trip but with bigger wildlife, bigger mountains, and a connecting scenic drive that's a destination in itself.

## Best Timing for Late Summer 2026

### The Sweet Spot: August 18 – September 7

| Factor | Late July (Jul 20-31) | Early-Mid August (Aug 1-17) | Late Aug – Early Sep (Aug 18 – Sep 7) | Mid September (Sep 8+) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Crowds** | Peak season, very heavy | Heavy but starting to ease | Noticeably lighter — schools back in session | Light, but services closing |
| **Weather** | Warm days (70-80s), afternoon thunderstorms common | Warm, thunderstorms | Warm days (60-75), cool nights (30-40s), fewer storms | Cold nights possible below freezing, variable weather |
| **Beartooth Hwy** | Open (typically opens late May) | Open | Open | Usually open through mid-September, weather dependent |
| **Wildfire smoke** | Possible | Most likely period for smoke | Still possible but often clears | Usually clear |
| **Wildlife** | Active, bears in high country | Active | Excellent — elk rut begins late Sep, bears fattening | Elk rut in full swing, bison rut winding down |
| **Wildflowers** | Peak at high elevations | Fading at lower, peak at high | Past peak but still present in alpine | Mostly done |
| **Trail conditions** | All trails open, some high snow patches | All trails open | All trails open, streams lower, easier crossings | All open, but early snow possible at high elevation |

**Recommendation:** Target **August 20 – September 1, 2026** (or the surrounding week). This gives you:
- Beartooth Highway reliably open
- Crowds dropping sharply as school starts (most schools resume Aug 18-25)
- Warm enough for comfortable camping but cool enough for big hiking days
- Excellent wildlife viewing (bears actively foraging, bison rut)
- Lower wildfire smoke risk than peak August

**Wildfire smoke caveat:** August is the highest-risk month for wildfire smoke in the northern Rockies. This is unpredictable — some years are clear, others are socked in for weeks. There is no way to plan around this. Check [AirNow](https://www.airnow.gov/) and [fire.airnow.gov](https://fire.airnow.gov/) in the days before your trip. If smoke is bad in Yellowstone, it may be clear in the Tetons (or vice versa) — the loop gives you flexibility.

### Beartooth Highway Season

- **Typical opening:** Late May (Memorial Day weekend target)
- **Typical closing:** Mid-October, but can close earlier with early snowstorms
- **Late August/early September:** Reliably open. The road occasionally closes briefly for summer snowstorms at the summit, but these are short-lived (hours, not days).
- **Status check:** Montana DOT road conditions — 511mt.net or call 511 in Montana
- [VERIFY 2026] Check Montana DOT for 2026 opening date: https://www.511mt.net/

## What Each Area Offers

### Yellowstone National Park

**Why it matters for you:** The world's largest concentration of geothermal features, massive wildlife (grizzlies, wolves, bison, elk), and a backcountry that 95% of visitors never touch. You can see Old Faithful with the crowds and then disappear into 1,000+ miles of trails where you won't see another person.

**Highlights relevant to your profile:**

| Category | What's There | Why It Fits You |
|---|---|---|
| **Big day hikes** | Avalanche Peak (4 mi, 2,100 ft gain), Electric Peak (20 mi, 5,000 ft gain), Mt Washburn (6.4 mi, 1,400 ft gain), Specimen Ridge (19 mi traverse), Bechler River Trail | You want 10+ mile days with real elevation — Yellowstone delivers. Electric Peak is a genuine mountaineering day hike. |
| **Solitude trails** | Specimen Ridge, Lamar River trail, Black Canyon of the Yellowstone, Bechler region (southwest corner) | The Lamar Valley and Bechler are where the crowds vanish. Specimen Ridge gives you petrified forests with nobody around. |
| **Wildlife** | Lamar Valley (wolves, bison, bears), Hayden Valley (bison, bears), Dunraven Pass (bears) | Lamar Valley at dawn is the best wildlife viewing in the lower 48. Period. You will see wolves, bison, and likely grizzlies. |
| **Geothermal** | Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic, Norris Geyser Basin, Mammoth Hot Springs | Worth seeing even as "tourist" stops. Grand Prismatic from the overlook trail is genuinely awe-inspiring. Norris is the hottest and most dynamic. |
| **Backpacking** | Bechler River, Lamar River, Yellowstone River / Black Canyon, Heart Lake | First time in grizzly country — Yellowstone backcountry is serious grizzly habitat. The Bechler River route hits waterfalls unreachable by day hike. |
| **Bouldering** | Limited inside the park. Gallatin Canyon (between Bozeman and West Yellowstone) has roadside bouldering. | Not the primary draw, but Gallatin Canyon bouldering is on the route if coming from Bozeman. |

**Time needed:** 2-3 full days minimum to hit the highlights. 3 days lets you do one big hike day, one wildlife/geothermal day, and one backcountry or solitude day.

### Beartooth Highway

**Why it matters for you:** This is not just a drive — it's a destination. The highest paved road in the northern Rockies, it traverses genuine alpine tundra with dozens of glacial lakes, wildflower meadows, and trailheads that access terrain above treeline with almost no one around.

**Highlights relevant to your profile:**

| Category | What's There | Why It Fits You |
|---|---|---|
| **Alpine hiking** | Beartooth Plateau trails, Clay Butte lookout, Glacier Lake, Beauty Lake, Beartooth High Lakes trail | This is alpine tundra hiking above 10,000 ft. No trees, 360-degree views, and you can wander cross-country on open terrain. Extremely low crowds. |
| **The drive itself** | 68 miles, 20+ switchbacks, summit at 10,947 ft | Charles Kuralt called it "the most beautiful drive in America." You'll understand why. Budget 3-4 hours to drive it with stops. |
| **Roadside stops** | Rock Creek Vista Point, Top of the World Store, alpine lakes pullouts | Short walks from pullouts reach glacial lakes and overlooks that would cost 10 miles of hiking elsewhere. |
| **Mountain biking** | Red Lodge has trail systems (West Fork trails) | If he wants to ride, Red Lodge is a legit mountain town with rental shops and trails. |
| **Gateway town** | Red Lodge, MT — at the eastern base of the highway | Genuine small Montana mountain town. Good food, breweries (Red Lodge Ales), less touristy than West Yellowstone or Jackson. |

**Time needed:** Half a day for the drive alone (it's only 68 miles but you'll stop constantly). A full day if you want to hike on the plateau. If Red Lodge appeals, an evening there is worth it.

### Grand Teton National Park

**Why it matters for you:** The Tetons are the most dramatic mountain scenery in the lower 48. No foothills — the range just explodes out of the valley floor. The hiking here is steeper, more alpine, and more technical than Yellowstone. This is where you push yourselves.

**Highlights relevant to your profile:**

| Category | What's There | Why It Fits You |
|---|---|---|
| **Big day hikes** | Paintbrush-Cascade Canyon loop (19.5 mi, 4,350 ft gain), Table Mountain (12 mi, 4,100 ft gain), Avalanche Divide (10 mi, 3,400 ft gain), Static Peak Divide (15 mi, 4,700 ft gain), Lake Solitude via Cascade Canyon (14.2 mi, 2,700 ft gain) | The Paintbrush-Cascade loop is one of the best day hikes in North America. Table Mountain gives you the iconic Teton view that Ansel Adams photographed. These are serious days — right in your wheelhouse. |
| **Solitude** | Avalanche Divide, Alaska Basin (from Teton Canyon on the west side), Death Canyon Shelf, Garnet Canyon | Death Canyon Shelf is a hanging valley above the world. Alaska Basin from the west side avoids the crowds entering from the east. |
| **Bouldering** | Teton Boulder Park (near Driggs, ID, west side of Tetons) — world-class granite bouldering. Also scattered bouldering near Jenny Lake. | Teton Boulder Park has classic problems V0-V12 on beautiful granite. This is a real bouldering destination. Pad rental in Driggs at Yostmark Mountain Equipment. |
| **Backpacking** | Teton Crest Trail (40 mi point-to-point), Death Canyon to Alaska Basin, Paintbrush Divide | The Teton Crest Trail is a bucket list route. Even doing a 2-night section (e.g., Death Canyon Shelf to Alaska Basin) would be unforgettable. Permits required. |
| **Scenic** | Schwabacher Landing (sunrise), Mormon Row (iconic barns with Teton backdrop), Jenny Lake, String Lake | These are the famous views. Worth seeing, but you'll earn better ones on the trails. |
| **Jackson Hole** | Town of Jackson — restaurants, breweries, gear shops | Jackson is expensive and touristy, but has great food and craft beer. Snake River Brewing is excellent. Melvin Brewing (previously in Alpine, now distributed). |

**Time needed:** 2-3 full days. The Paintbrush-Cascade loop alone takes a full day. Add a day for another big hike or bouldering day, plus time for scenic stops.

## Airport Options and How They Shape the Route

### Option 1: Bozeman Yellowstone International (BZN)

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Bozeman, MT — 90 miles north of Yellowstone's north entrance (Gardiner) |
| **Drive to Yellowstone** | 1.5 hours to Gardiner (north entrance); 1.5 hours to West Yellowstone (west entrance) |
| **Airlines from BOS** | Delta (connection via MSP or SLC), United (connection via DEN or ORD), Alaska, JetBlue (seasonal — check 2026) |
| **Typical RT price range** | $350-600 from BOS **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Rental cars** | All major agencies at the airport. Enterprise, Hertz, Budget, National, Avis, etc. |
| **Pros** | Usually cheapest flights. Bigger airport with more flight options. Natural starting point for clockwise loop (Yellowstone north first). Bozeman itself has good food/beer scene. |
| **Cons** | 90-minute drive to start the park. You end the loop at Jackson Hole and have to get back to Bozeman (5+ hours via the parks, or 6 hours via Idaho on US-20). |
| **Best for** | Clockwise loop with open-jaw rental, or if you're OK doing a long drive on last day back to BZN. |

### Option 2: Jackson Hole Airport (JAC)

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Inside Grand Teton National Park — the only commercial airport inside a national park |
| **Drive to Tetons** | 0 minutes — you're literally in the park when you land |
| **Drive to Yellowstone** | 1 hour to Yellowstone's south entrance; 2.5 hours to Lamar Valley |
| **Airlines from BOS** | United (connection via DEN), American (connection via DFW or ORD), Delta (connection via SLC) |
| **Typical RT price range** | $450-800+ from BOS — generally $100-200 more than BZN **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Rental cars** | Available but limited selection and higher prices than BZN. Book early. |
| **Pros** | Zero wasted time getting to the parks. Start in the Tetons immediately. Loop naturally ends back in Jackson. No long repositioning drive. |
| **Cons** | Significantly more expensive flights and rental cars. Fewer flight options. The airport is small and can have weather delays. |
| **Best for** | Counterclockwise loop (Tetons first, Yellowstone, Beartooth, then back to Jackson via Cody/east route — but this adds driving). Or out-and-back: Tetons → Yellowstone → Beartooth → back to Jackson. |

### Option 3: Open-Jaw (Fly into one, out of the other)

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **How it works** | Book a multi-city flight: BOS → BZN, then JAC → BOS (or vice versa) |
| **Typical RT price range** | Sometimes comparable to round-trip BZN; sometimes $50-150 more **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Rental car** | One-way rental fee applies — typically $50-200 extra depending on company **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Pros** | No backtracking. The loop is truly a loop. Every mile you drive is new scenery. Saves 5-6 hours of driving vs. returning to your origin airport. |
| **Cons** | More complex booking. One-way rental car fee. Fewer flight options for the multi-city routing. |
| **Best for** | Maximizing time in the parks and minimizing wasted driving. This is the enthusiast's choice. |

### Recommendation

**Open-jaw (BZN in, JAC out) is the strongest option** if the price premium is modest (check multi-city fares on Google Flights). You eliminate 5+ hours of dead driving, and every mile of the trip is forward progress through new terrain. The one-way rental fee is worth it.

**If open-jaw pricing is ugly**, fly round-trip into BZN and accept the long drive back on the last day (Tetons → BZN via US-26 to US-20 through Idaho, roughly 5 hours — or back through Yellowstone, roughly 5 hours via the parks).

**JAC round-trip** makes sense only if you want to do an out-and-back rather than a true loop, or if a great flight deal appears.

**Where to book flights:**
- Google Flights: https://www.google.com/flights — best for comparing across airlines and finding open-jaw pricing
- Check direct with airlines for BOS routes: Delta (delta.com), United (united.com), American (aa.com), JetBlue (jetblue.com)
- Set fare alerts 4-6 months out (by March-April 2026 for late August travel)

## Route Options with Trade-offs

### Option A: Clockwise Loop — BZN to JAC (Recommended)

**Route:** Bozeman → North Entrance (Gardiner) → Yellowstone interior → Northeast Entrance → Beartooth Highway → Red Lodge → back over Beartooth → Northeast Entrance → south through Yellowstone → Grand Tetons → Jackson Hole

**Or with open-jaw:** Bozeman → ... → Grand Tetons → fly out of Jackson Hole

```
Day flow: BZN → Gardiner → Mammoth/Lamar → Beartooth → back into YNP →
          Canyon/Lake → South to Tetons → Jenny Lake area → Jackson → JAC
```

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| Natural north-to-south flow | Beartooth is mid-trip, requiring a drive out and back (unless you stay in Red Lodge) |
| Start with Lamar Valley wildlife at dawn on day 1 | If doing round-trip BZN, the last-day drive back is long |
| Build toward the Tetons as the grand finale | Requires driving the NE entrance road twice (Cooke City to Tower Junction) |
| Open-jaw BZN→JAC eliminates backtracking | |
| Lamar Valley + Beartooth are geographically clustered | |

**Best for:** Open-jaw travelers, or those who want to end the trip with the dramatic Tetons.

### Option B: Counterclockwise Loop — JAC Round-Trip

**Route:** Jackson Hole → Grand Tetons → south into Yellowstone → Yellowstone interior → Northeast Entrance → Beartooth Highway → Red Lodge → return to Jackson via Cody, WY

```
Day flow: JAC → Jenny Lake/Tetons → South entrance → Canyon/Lake →
          Mammoth/Lamar → Beartooth out-and-back or via Cody → back to JAC
```

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| No backtracking if you loop via Cody (east side) | Returning to Jackson via Cody adds 5+ hours of driving through non-park areas |
| Start with Tetons when you're freshest | More expensive flights to JAC |
| Can do Beartooth as a day trip from Cooke City area | You see the Tetons first — less dramatic buildup |
| Only one airport to deal with | Cody route is less scenic than the parks |

**Best for:** If JAC flights are well-priced, or if you want the simplicity of one airport.

### Option C: BZN Round-Trip with Beartooth Focus

**Route:** Bozeman → Beartooth Highway (via Red Lodge) → into Yellowstone NE entrance → Yellowstone interior → south to Tetons → back to Bozeman

```
Day flow: BZN → Red Lodge → Beartooth Hwy → Cooke City → Lamar →
          Canyon/Yellowstone → South to Tetons → Jackson → BZN
```

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| Cheapest flights (BZN round-trip) | Last day is a 5-hour dead drive from Jackson back to Bozeman |
| Hit Beartooth first while energy is highest | That last-day drive wastes a half-day of trip time |
| Red Lodge is a great first-night stop | Route is less of a natural loop |
| Gallatin Canyon bouldering on the first or last day | |

**Best for:** Budget-conscious travelers willing to trade a half-day of driving for lower airfare.

### Recommendation

**Option A (Clockwise, BZN → JAC open-jaw)** is the strongest choice for you. It eliminates wasted driving, flows naturally through the landscape, and lets you build toward the Tetons as the dramatic finale of the trip. The Tetons' steep alpine hiking on your last full days — when you're fully acclimatized and in trail shape — makes sense physically too.

If open-jaw pricing doesn't work out, **Option C (BZN round-trip)** is the practical fallback. Just accept the long drive back and plan it as a decompression day.

## Proposed Itinerary Skeletons

These are day-allocation frameworks, not rigid schedules. Each day's specific activities would come from the hiking-trails and activity features.

### Skeleton 1: The 7-Day Classic (Recommended)

Open-jaw BZN in → JAC out. Balanced time across all three areas.

| Day | Area | Focus | Driving | Camp Location |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 0** | Travel | Fly BOS → BZN. Pick up rental car, buy bear spray. Drive to Gardiner or camp near north entrance. | 1.5 hr from BZN | Gardiner area or Eagle Creek CG |
| **Day 1** | Yellowstone North | Dawn in Lamar Valley for wildlife. Day hike: Specimen Ridge, Slough Creek, or Black Canyon. Evening at Mammoth Hot Springs. | Minimal in-park | Slough Creek CG or Tower Fall CG |
| **Day 2** | Beartooth Highway | Drive NE entrance → Red Lodge via Beartooth Hwy. Hike on the Beartooth Plateau (alpine lakes, tundra). Evening in Red Lodge — dinner + brewery. | 2.5 hr (scenic) | Red Lodge (commercial campground) or Beartooth Lake CG (USFS, on the highway) |
| **Day 3** | Yellowstone Core | Drive back over Beartooth → NE entrance → Canyon/Lake area. Grand Prismatic, Norris Geyser Basin, Upper/Lower Falls. Optional hike: Avalanche Peak or Mt. Washburn. | 3 hr total | Canyon Village CG or Bridge Bay CG |
| **Day 4** | Yellowstone South / Transition | Morning hike in Yellowstone (Mystic Falls, Fairy Falls + Grand Prismatic overlook, or Bechler area). Afternoon drive south through Tetons. | 2 hr | Jenny Lake CG (if you score a spot — FCFS, fills by 8 AM) or Gros Ventre CG |
| **Day 5** | Grand Tetons — Big Day | The big one: Paintbrush-Cascade Canyon loop (19.5 mi, 4,350 ft gain) or Table Mountain or Static Peak Divide. | Minimal | Same camp as Day 4 |
| **Day 6** | Grand Tetons — Flex Day | Options: Bouldering at Teton Boulder Park (drive to Driggs), second hike (Death Canyon, Lake Solitude, Avalanche Divide), or canoeing/kayaking on Jenny or String Lake. Evening in Jackson — dinner, Snake River Brewing. | Variable | Same camp or move to Jackson area |
| **Day 7** | Departure | Morning at Schwabacher Landing for sunrise + Teton reflection photos. Fly out of JAC. | Minimal | Fly home |

**Total driving:** ~10-12 hours across the week (spread across 4 driving days)
**Big hike days:** 3-4 (Days 1, 2, 5 are the biggest)
**Rest/flex days:** Day 4 and Day 6 have flexibility built in

### Skeleton 2: The 8-Day Deep Dive

Same open-jaw structure but with an extra day, allowing a backcountry overnight.

| Day | Area | Focus | Camp Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 0** | Travel | Fly in, drive to Gardiner | Gardiner area |
| **Day 1** | Yellowstone North | Lamar Valley dawn. Big day hike (Specimen Ridge or Electric Peak). | Slough Creek CG |
| **Day 2** | Beartooth | Full day on Beartooth: drive + hike + Red Lodge evening | Red Lodge or Beartooth Lake CG |
| **Day 3** | Yellowstone Core | Geothermal (Grand Prismatic, Norris). Mt. Washburn or Avalanche Peak. | Canyon CG |
| **Day 4** | Yellowstone Backcountry | Backcountry overnight — Bechler River corridor or Heart Lake | Backcountry campsite (permit required) |
| **Day 5** | Backcountry → Tetons | Hike out in morning. Drive south to Tetons. Afternoon at Jenny/String Lake. | Jenny Lake CG or Gros Ventre CG |
| **Day 6** | Grand Tetons — Big Day | Paintbrush-Cascade loop or Table Mountain | Same camp |
| **Day 7** | Tetons Flex | Bouldering, second hike, or Jackson | Same camp or Jackson area |
| **Day 8** | Departure | Sunrise, fly out JAC | Fly home |

**Trade-off vs. Skeleton 1:** You gain a backcountry night (bucket-list experience, first grizzly-country overnight) but need an extra day off work and need to secure a backcountry permit in advance.

### Skeleton 3: The 6-Day Efficient

For a shorter trip — compressed but still hits all three areas.

| Day | Area | Focus | Camp Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 0** | Travel | Fly in to BZN, drive to park | Gardiner or Mammoth area |
| **Day 1** | Yellowstone | Lamar Valley dawn, then south: Grand Prismatic, Norris. One big hike. | Canyon or Bridge Bay CG |
| **Day 2** | Beartooth | NE entrance → Beartooth Hwy → alpine hike → Red Lodge or return to park | Red Lodge or Slough Creek CG |
| **Day 3** | Yellowstone → Tetons | Morning at Yellowstone Lake area. Afternoon drive south to Tetons. | Jenny Lake or Gros Ventre CG |
| **Day 4** | Grand Tetons | All-in: Paintbrush-Cascade loop or Table Mountain. | Same camp |
| **Day 5** | Tetons Flex / Departure prep | Half day hike or bouldering. Evening in Jackson. | Jackson area |
| **Day 6** | Departure | Fly out JAC (or drive to BZN if round-trip) | Fly home |

**Trade-off:** You see everything but at a faster pace. Yellowstone gets compressed to 1.5 days, which means you choose between geothermal and backcountry — you can't do both deeply. Fine for a "greatest hits" trip but less of the going-deep experience you value.

### Recommendation

**Skeleton 1 (7-Day Classic)** is the sweet spot. It gives you enough time to go deep in each area without feeling rushed, includes flex days for weather/energy adjustment, and doesn't require backcountry permits (reducing booking complexity). If you can swing 8 days, Skeleton 2 with a backcountry night would be unforgettable — but only if you're confident about the permit and grizzly-country overnight readiness.

## Summary: Key Decision Points

Before moving to detailed feature research, you need to resolve these questions:

| Decision | Options | What Drives It |
|---|---|---|
| **Airport strategy** | Open-jaw (BZN→JAC), BZN round-trip, or JAC round-trip | Flight prices and one-way rental car availability. Check Google Flights for all three configurations. |
| **Trip length** | 6, 7, or 8 days on the ground | How much time off work you can take. 7 days is the sweet spot. |
| **Backcountry overnight** | Include a 1-night backcountry trip or stay car-camping only | Comfort level with first grizzly-country overnight + permit availability. Can decide later — apply for permits now and decide closer to trip. |
| **Loop direction** | Clockwise (north → east → south) or counterclockwise | Airport choice largely determines this. Clockwise from BZN is most natural. |
| **When exactly** | Late August vs. early September | Both are good. Late Aug has slightly warmer weather; early Sep has fewer people and early fall colors. Check smoke forecasts closer to trip. |
