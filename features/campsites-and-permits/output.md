## Yellowstone NP Campgrounds

Yellowstone has 12 frontcountry campgrounds. Some are operated by the NPS directly, others by the park concessioner (currently Yellowstone National Park Lodges / Xanterra). The concessioner-operated campgrounds tend to be larger with more amenities and take reservations; some NPS campgrounds are first-come, first-served (FCFS) only.

### Reservable Campgrounds

These campgrounds accept advance reservations through **recreation.gov**. They are the backbone of your camping strategy — book these first.

### First-Come, First-Served (FCFS) Campgrounds

These campgrounds do NOT take reservations. You show up, check the self-registration board, and grab a site if one is open. They tend to be smaller, more rustic, and in more interesting locations — but require strategy to secure a spot.

#### Slough Creek Campground

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Northeast Yellowstone, up a 2.3-mile unpaved road off the Northeast Entrance Road. In the heart of wolf and grizzly country, near Lamar Valley. |
| **Sites** | ~16 sites (very small) |
| **Reservation** | **FCFS only** — no reservations **[VERIFY 2026 — NPS has been gradually converting some FCFS campgrounds to reservable]** |
| **Price** | ~$20/night **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Season** | Mid-June – early October (depending on road conditions) |
| **Elevation** | ~6,250 ft |
| **Amenities** | Vault toilets, potable water. No other amenities. Very rustic. |
| **Pros** | **The best campground in Yellowstone for your trip.** Right in the Lamar Valley ecosystem. Wolves howl at night. Grizzlies are frequently seen near the campground. The Slough Creek trail from here is one of the best in the park — wide meadows, cutthroat trout fishing, solitude. True wilderness feel. |
| **Cons** | Only 16 sites — fills extremely early in peak season (by 8-9 AM, sometimes earlier). Unpaved access road. No hookups or amenities. Bear management area — strict food storage required. |
| **FCFS strategy** | Arrive by **7:00 AM or earlier** in late August. Check the board for checkout times — sites turn over when people leave, usually between 8-11 AM. Be ready to wait. Weekdays are easier than weekends. Have a backup plan (Tower Fall or Canyon). |
| **Best for itinerary** | Day 1 — if you can snag it. Camp here to be in position for dawn wildlife in Lamar Valley. Worth the effort. |

## Grand Teton NP Campgrounds

Grand Teton has six frontcountry campgrounds, a mix of reservable and FCFS. Jenny Lake is the crown jewel but is tiny and FCFS-only — getting a site there requires dedication.

### Reservable Campgrounds

#### Signal Mountain Campground

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Location** | On the shore of Jackson Lake, with Teton views. Central Grand Teton NP. |
| **Sites** | ~81 sites |
| **Reservation** | Yes — via recreation.gov |
| **Reservation opening** | Typically opens on a rolling window or a specific date in winter/spring for the full summer season **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Price** | ~$40–$45/night **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Season** | Early May – mid-October |
| **Elevation** | ~6,800 ft |

| **Cons** | Relatively small (81 sites), books up quickly. More expensive than FCFS options. |
| **Best for itinerary** | Days 5-6 — your Grand Teton base camp. Excellent setting for the celebration vibe you're after. |

### First-Come, First-Served (FCFS) Campgrounds

#### Jenny Lake Campground

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Location** | On the shore of Jenny Lake, at the base of the Teton Range. THE prime location in Grand Teton NP. |
| **Sites** | ~49 sites — **tents only** (no RVs, no trailers) |
| **Reservation** | **FCFS only** — no reservations **[VERIFY 2026 — NPS may eventually convert this, but as of recent years it remains FCFS]** |
| **Price** | ~$35/night **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Season** | Early May – late September |
| **Elevation** | ~6,800 ft |
| **Max stay** | 7 nights **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Amenities** | Flush toilets, potable water. No showers (nearest at Signal Mountain Lodge or Colter Bay). Jenny Lake Visitor Center and general store nearby. |
| **Pros** | **The single best campground location in Grand Teton NP.** You are literally at the base of the Tetons, steps from Jenny Lake, and within walking/biking distance of Cascade Canyon and Paintbrush Canyon trailheads. Wake up to cathedral peaks. Tents-only means a quieter, more intimate vibe. This is the campground that matches your "going deeper" and "celebration" values. |
| **Cons** | Only 49 sites and insanely popular. In peak summer, it fills by 7-8 AM (sometimes earlier). Late August gives you a slightly better shot as families with school-age kids clear out. No reservations = pure hustle. |

The checkout time is typically 11 AM but some people leave earlier. If you don't get a site on first pass, circle back around 10-11 AM when checkouts happen. Weekdays (Tue-Thu) in late August are your best bet. Have Gros Ventre as your backup. |
| **Best for itinerary** | Days 5-6 — your Grand Teton base. If you get it, this is the dream camp. Worth losing an hour of sleep to try. |

|---|---|

| **Sites** | ~300+ sites (one of the largest in the Teton area) |
| **Reservation** | **Partially reservable, partially FCFS** — this has varied by year. Some loops may be reservable via recreation.gov while others remain FCFS. **[VERIFY 2026]** |

| **Elevation** | ~6,600 ft |

| **Cons** | Less dramatic setting than Jenny Lake or Signal Mountain — you're in sagebrush flats, not at the base of the peaks. Further from the main Teton trailheads. Can feel exposed and hot. Bison sometimes roam through (exciting but keep distance). |
| **FCFS strategy** | You can usually roll in by early-to-mid afternoon and find a site. It's the "safety valve" of Grand Teton camping. |
| **Best for itinerary** | Reliable backup for Days 4-6 if Jenny Lake and Signal Mountain don't work out. Also fine as a first-night Teton camp if you arrive in the afternoon from Yellowstone. |

## Beartooth Highway / Red Lodge Area Campgrounds

The Beartooth Highway (US-212) runs through Custer Gallatin and Shoshone National Forests. Several USFS campgrounds sit right on or near the highway at high elevation. Red Lodge, MT, at the eastern base, has both USFS and commercial options.

### USFS Campgrounds Along Beartooth Highway

These are managed by the US Forest Service. Most are FCFS; some may take reservations through recreation.gov. They're rustic, beautiful, and at high elevation (9,000-10,000 ft — bring warm sleeping bags).

#### Beartooth Lake Campground (Shoshone NF)

| **Price** | ~$15–$20/night **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Season** | July – mid-September (short season due to elevation) |
| **Amenities** | Vault toilets, hand-pumped water, boat launch. No hookups. |

| **Cons** | Cold at night, even in August (can drop to 20s-30s F). Very limited sites. No cell service. |
| **Best for itinerary** | Day 2 — if you want to camp on the Beartooth Plateau rather than descending to Red Lodge. Spectacular but cold. |

## Backcountry Permits — Yellowstone

If you decide to include a 1-2 night backcountry trip (Skeleton 2 from the overview), you'll need a backcountry use permit.

### How the System Works

Yellowstone uses a **two-phase system** for backcountry permits:

**Phase 1: Advance Reservations**

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **When to apply** | Applications typically accepted starting in **early-to-mid March** for the upcoming summer season **[VERIFY 2026 — check nps.gov/yell for exact opening date]** |
| **How to apply** | Online through recreation.gov (Yellowstone transitioned from a mail/fax system to online in recent years) **[VERIFY 2026 — the system has been evolving]** |
| **Fee** | $25 reservation fee per trip + $10/person/night (approximately) **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **What you specify** | Exact campsites and dates for each night. Yellowstone backcountry has ~300 designated campsites, each with a unique ID (e.g., "1Y9" for a site on the Yellowstone River). |
| **Confirmation** | You receive a permit confirmation. Must pick up the actual permit in person at a backcountry office within 48 hours of your trip start. |
| **Availability** | Popular sites (Shoshone Lake, Heart Lake, Bechler River corridor) fill quickly during advance reservations. Less popular areas are easier. |

**Phase 2: Walk-Up Permits**

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **When available** | Starting June 1 (approximately) — permits available in person 48 hours before your trip start date |
| **Where to get them** | Backcountry offices at Canyon, Mammoth, Old Faithful, Grant Village, Tower, and other ranger stations |
| **Fee** | Same as advance ($10/person/night) but no reservation fee |
| **Strategy** | Show up at a backcountry office first thing in the morning, 1-2 days before you want to go. Tell the ranger your preferred route and ask what's available. Be flexible on exact sites. |
| **Pros** | No advance planning needed. You can decide on the ground based on weather and energy. Rangers are incredibly helpful and will suggest itineraries. |
| **Cons** | Popular sites may be taken. You need to be flexible. |

### Recommended Backcountry Areas for Your Itinerary

If you decide to do a backcountry overnight, here are the best options that fit the loop itinerary:

#### Option 1: Heart Lake (1 night)

## Backcountry Permits — Grand Teton

Grand Teton has a separate backcountry permit system from Yellowstone.

### How the System Works

**Advance Reservations**

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **When to apply** | Advance reservation applications are typically accepted starting **January 10** for the upcoming summer season. One-third of all backcountry sites are available for advance reservation; two-thirds are held for walk-ups. **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **How to apply** | Online through recreation.gov **[VERIFY 2026 — Grand Teton has been transitioning systems]** |
| **Fee** | $45 reservation fee per trip (non-refundable) + there may be a per-night fee **[VERIFY 2026]** |
| **Confirmation** | Advance reservation must be converted to an actual permit in person at a permit office (Craig Thomas Discovery & Visitor Center in Moose, or Jenny Lake Ranger Station). Must be picked up by 10 AM on the day your trip starts, or the reservation is forfeited. |

**Walk-Up Permits**

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **When available** | Walk-up permits are available starting the day before your trip, on a first-come, first-served basis |
| **Where to get them** | Craig Thomas Discovery & Visitor Center (Moose) — primary office. Also Jenny Lake Ranger Station and Colter Bay Visitor Center. |
| **When to show up** | Permit offices open early (7-8 AM). For popular zones in peak summer, people line up before opening. Late August gives you better odds. |
| **Strategy** | Be flexible on your camping zones. The most popular areas (Cascade Canyon camping zones, Paintbrush area, Death Canyon Shelf, Alaska Basin) fill for walk-ups, but rangers can usually find you something if you're flexible. |

### Recommended Backcountry Areas for Your Itinerary

#### Option 1: Cascade Canyon to Paintbrush Divide (1-2 nights)

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|

| **Camping zones** | Death Canyon Shelf, Alaska Basin (Jedediah Smith Wilderness — no quota, just follow Leave No Trace) |

## Park Entrance Fees and Passes

### America the Beautiful Pass — The Clear Winner

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Cost** | $80 per pass |
| **Coverage** | Unlimited access to all National Parks, National Forests, BLM lands, National Wildlife Refuges, and other federal lands for 12 months from date of purchase |
| **How many people** | Covers the passholder + all passengers in a single private vehicle at per-vehicle fee areas. For per-person fee areas, covers the passholder + 3 additional adults. |
| **Valid at** | Yellowstone NP, Grand Teton NP, all USFS lands (Beartooth Highway passes through National Forest), and 2,000+ other federal recreation sites |
| **Purchase** | Online at https://store.usgs.gov/pass/index.html, at any park entrance station, or at REI/other outdoor retailers |

### Individual Park Fees for Comparison

| Park | Vehicle Fee (7-day) | Per-Person Fee | Annual Park Pass |
|---|---|---|---|
| Yellowstone NP | $35 | $20 | $70 (Yellowstone only) |
| Grand Teton NP | $35 | $20 | $70 (Grand Teton only) |
| **Combined** | **$70 total** | **$40/person** | **$140 total** |

### The Math

- **America the Beautiful Pass:** $80 for both parks (and everything else for a year)
- **Individual vehicle fees:** $35 + $35 = $70 for both parks
- **Savings from the pass:** The pass is only $10 more than paying individually, AND it's valid for 12 months at every federal recreation site. If you visit any federal land between now and next year, it pays for itself immediately.

**Recommendation:** Buy the America the Beautiful Pass. At $80, it's essentially the same price as individual park fees and gives you year-round access to federal lands everywhere. Buy it online before the trip or at the first park entrance station.
