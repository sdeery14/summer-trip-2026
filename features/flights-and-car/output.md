## Flight Options: BOS to BZN

Bozeman Yellowstone International (BZN) is the larger, better-connected, and typically cheaper airport for accessing the Greater Yellowstone area. It has seen massive growth in recent years and now has extensive service.

## Flight Options: BOS to JAC

Jackson Hole Airport (JAC) is the only commercial airport located inside a national park (Grand Teton). It is smaller, serves fewer airlines, and is consistently more expensive than BZN.

## Open-Jaw Flights

An open-jaw itinerary means you fly into one airport and out of another: BOS -> BZN, then JAC -> BOS (or the reverse). This eliminates the 5+ hour dead drive from Jackson back to Bozeman on your last day.

### How to Book Open-Jaw Flights

**Google Flights (best tool for this):**
1. Go to [google.com/flights](https://www.google.com/flights)
2. Click the round-trip dropdown and select **"Multi-city"**
3. Enter Flight 1: BOS -> BZN, Aug 20 (or your date)
4. Enter Flight 2: JAC -> BOS, Aug 27 (or your date)
5. Search -- Google Flights will show combined pricing for both legs

**Directly with airlines:**
- **Delta, United, American** all allow multi-city bookings on their websites. Look for "Multi-city" or "Multiple destinations" option on the flight search page.
- **Booking tip:** Sometimes booking two separate one-way tickets is cheaper than a multi-city fare. Google Flights will show you if this is the case -- compare the multi-city price against the sum of two one-ways.

**Third-party sites:**
- Kayak.com, Expedia, and Skiplagged all support multi-city search
- **Caution:** Third-party bookings can complicate changes and cancellations. Booking directly with the airline is safer, especially for summer travel when weather delays are possible.

### Typical Open-Jaw Pricing **[VERIFY 2026]**

| Configuration | Estimated Total (2 passengers) | Comparison to RT BZN |
|---|---|---|
| BOS -> BZN + JAC -> BOS (multi-city) | $800-1,200 total for two | $0-200 more than RT BZN for two |
| BOS -> JAC + BZN -> BOS (reverse) | $800-1,300 total for two | Similar or slightly more |
| Two separate one-ways (BOS->BZN + JAC->BOS) | $750-1,400 total for two | Sometimes cheaper, sometimes not |

**Key insight:** Open-jaw pricing is often surprisingly close to round-trip BZN pricing because one leg (BOS-BZN) is cheap while the other (JAC-BOS) is expensive, but they average out. The delta vs. round-trip BZN is often only $50-150 total for two people -- easily worth it to save 5+ hours of driving.

### Which Direction? BZN First vs JAC First

| Factor | BZN In / JAC Out (Recommended) | JAC In / BZN Out |
|---|---|---|
| **Natural loop flow** | Clockwise: Bozeman -> Yellowstone North -> Beartooth -> Yellowstone South -> Tetons -> Jackson | Counterclockwise: Tetons -> Yellowstone South -> North -> Beartooth -> Bozeman |
| **First day** | Land in BZN, 90-min drive to Yellowstone north entrance. Buy bear spray in Bozeman on the way. | Land in JAC, immediately in the Tetons. Less driving but may be disoriented. |
| **Last day** | Drop car at JAC, which is literally inside Grand Teton NP. Quick morning at Schwabacher Landing, then fly out. | Need to drive from wherever you are back to BZN -- could be 1.5-5 hours depending on your last camp. |
| **Trip narrative** | Build from Yellowstone's breadth to Beartooth's drama to the Tetons' grand finale. | Start with the big mountains, then explore Yellowstone -- less of a dramatic build. |
| **Flight prices** | BOS->BZN is the cheaper leg first. | BOS->JAC is the expensive leg first. |

**Recommendation: BZN in, JAC out.** This matches the clockwise loop from the overview document and gives you the best narrative arc for the trip.

## Do You Need 4WD/AWD?

This is one of the most common questions for Yellowstone/Teton trips. The short answer: **AWD is nice to have but not strictly required for most of the trip.**

### Road Surface Analysis

| Road | Surface | 2WD OK? | AWD/4WD Recommended? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All main park roads (Grand Loop, Teton Park Road) | Paved | Yes | Not needed | Well-maintained paved roads. A sedan can handle these. |
| Beartooth Highway (US-212) | Paved | Yes | Not needed | Fully paved. Steep grades but any car can do it. |
| Teton Pass (WY-22, to Driggs for bouldering) | Paved | Yes | Not needed | Steep (10% grade) but paved and well-maintained. |
| Lamar Valley road (to Slough Creek TH) | Paved + short gravel | Yes | Helpful | Paved to the trailhead turnoff; short gravel section to parking. |
| Slough Creek Campground road | Gravel, 2 miles | Careful 2WD | Recommended | Washboarded gravel. Clearance matters more than AWD. |
| Gravel Creek Road (Bechler area) | Gravel/dirt, unpaved | Careful 2WD | Recommended | Remote area. Can be muddy after rain. |
| Shadow Mountain Road (Teton bouldering access) | Dirt | Careful 2WD | Recommended | Depends on conditions. Dry = fine. Wet = dicey in 2WD. |
| Most trailhead parking areas | Gravel lots | Yes | Not needed | Standard gravel parking lots. |
| Gros Ventre Road (to campground) | Paved + gravel | Yes | Helpful | Mostly paved but some gravel sections. |

### Recommendation

**Book a midsize SUV (Subaru Outback, Toyota RAV4, Honda CR-V, Chevy Equinox class) with AWD if available.** Here is why:

1. **Ground clearance matters more than drivetrain.** The issue on dirt trailhead roads is not traction -- it is bottoming out. An SUV sits 7-8 inches off the ground vs 5-6 for a sedan. That makes the difference on washboard gravel.
2. **AWD is insurance, not a necessity.** You probably will not need it. But if it rains and a dirt road gets slick, or if you want to access a more remote trailhead, AWD means you do not have to think twice.
3. **Cargo space.** Two people's camping gear (tent, sleeping bags/pads, camp kitchen, cooler, bear canister, day packs, clothing for a week) fills a sedan trunk completely. An SUV gives you room to organize and access gear without unpacking everything.
4. **Cost difference is modest.** An AWD midsize SUV is typically only $50-100/week more than a sedan.

**You do NOT need a full-size SUV or a Jeep.** A RAV4 or Outback class vehicle handles every road you will encounter on this trip. Save the money.

**Booking tip:** When searching, filter for "SUV" category. If AWD/4WD is important to you, call the agency after booking to request it specifically -- online booking often does not let you specify drivetrain, only vehicle class.

## One-Way Rental: BZN Pickup to JAC Dropoff

One-way rental is the key that makes the open-jaw flight strategy work. You pick up the car in Bozeman and drop it off in Jackson Hole, matching your BOS->BZN / JAC->BOS flight routing.

### Which Companies Allow One-Way BZN to JAC? **[VERIFY 2026]**

| Agency | One-Way BZN->JAC? | Typical Surcharge | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Enterprise/National/Alamo** | Yes | $50-200 | Enterprise Holdings (parent company) generally allows one-way between any of their locations. Most flexible policy. Call to confirm BZN-JAC specifically. |
| **Hertz/Dollar/Thrifty** | Usually yes | $75-250 | Hertz Corp allows most one-way in-state and neighboring-state rentals. BZN (MT) to JAC (WY) crosses a state line -- confirm the fee. |
| **Avis/Budget** | Usually yes | $75-300 | Avis Budget Group allows one-way but fees vary by location and season. Summer in Yellowstone country = higher fees. |

**Important notes on one-way rentals:**

- **One-way fees are in addition to the base rental rate.** A $500/week SUV with a $150 one-way fee = $650 total.
- **Availability can be limited.** Agencies need to reposition cars back from JAC to BZN, so they may cap how many one-way rentals they offer per day. Book early.
- **Cross-state fees:** BZN is in Montana, JAC is in Wyoming. Some agencies charge extra for cross-state returns. Others do not. Ask specifically.
- **Drop-off hours:** JAC is a small airport. Confirm the rental return location accepts after-hours drop-off if your flight is early morning.

### How to Book a One-Way Rental

1. Go to any rental agency's website or aggregator (Kayak, Costco Travel, AutoSlash)
2. Select **"Different drop-off location"** or **"One-way"**
3. Enter: Pick-up: Bozeman, MT (BZN airport), Drop-off: Jackson, WY (JAC airport)
4. Enter your dates
5. The quoted price will include the one-way fee (it is baked into the total, not shown separately on most sites)

**Best aggregators for one-way rental pricing:**

| Site | URL | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Costco Travel** | [costcotravel.com](https://www.costcotravel.com) | Often has the best rental car prices. Requires Costco membership. Includes second driver free with some agencies. |
| **AutoSlash** | [autoslash.com](https://www.autoslash.com) | Finds coupons and re-checks your rental for price drops. Great for one-way. |
| **Kayak** | [kayak.com/cars](https://www.kayak.com/cars) | Good comparison tool. Shows one-way fees clearly. |
| **Direct with Enterprise** | [enterprise.com](https://www.enterprise.com) | Enterprise tends to have the lowest one-way fees and best availability for this route. Start here. |

## Recommendation

**Strategy A (Open-Jaw: BOS->BZN, JAC->BOS) is the best choice** for this trip, and here is why:

1. **Time is your most valuable resource.** You have ~7 days. Spending 5-6 hours driving back to Bozeman on your last day costs you a full half-day of the trip. The open-jaw premium of ~$200 buys you that time back.

2. **The loop flows naturally clockwise.** BZN -> Yellowstone North -> Beartooth -> Yellowstone South -> Tetons -> JAC follows the route laid out in the overview document. Every mile you drive is new terrain and new experiences.

3. **The price difference is modest.** The midpoint estimate for Strategy A ($1,600) vs Strategy B ($1,400) is about $200. That is $100 per person for 5+ hours of vacation time. This is exactly the kind of "spend smart" decision the vision document describes -- pay for the thing that makes the trip better.

4. **Risk mitigation:** The main risk is the JAC->BOS return flight. JAC is a small airport and weather delays happen. Mitigate this by:
   - Flying out in the morning (afternoon weather is worse)
   - Having a backup plan: if your JAC flight is cancelled, you can rent another car and drive to BZN or SLC for alternate flights (SLC is 5 hours from Jackson)
   - Building a buffer: if your flight home is Wednesday, plan your last park day for Tuesday and camp near Jackson Tuesday night

**Fallback:** If open-jaw pricing is ugly (more than $300 above round-trip BZN for two people), go with Strategy B (round-trip BZN). Accept the drive back and treat it as a decompression day. Take US-191 through Gallatin Canyon rather than the Idaho route -- it is more scenic and passes bouldering areas she might enjoy as a last stop.

### Checked Bag Considerations

You are flying with camping gear. This matters for airline choice.

| Airline | First Checked Bag | Second Checked Bag | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Southwest** | Free | Free | Best for gear-heavy travel. No bag fees at all. |
| **JetBlue** | Free (Blue/Blue Plus) or $35 (Blue Basic) | $45 | Good if they fly BOS-BZN. |
| **Delta** | $35 | $45 | Standard. Can be waived with Delta credit card. |
| **United** | $35 | $45 | Standard. Can be waived with United credit card. |
| **American** | $35 | $45 | Standard. Can be waived with AA credit card. |

**Gear packing tip:** You probably need 2 checked bags per person (4 total) or 1 checked bag + 1 large carry-on each. Your bear canister does NOT need to fly -- buy bear spray at destination (cannot fly with it) and rent a canister if needed (or ship yours via UPS ground ahead of time). The canister itself can fly in checked luggage, though. Camp stove fuel (isobutane canisters) CANNOT fly -- buy at destination (available at any outdoor store in Bozeman or Jackson).
